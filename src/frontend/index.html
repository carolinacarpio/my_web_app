Let's Explore the project: Ekosky!

Thousands of years ago, our ancestors gazed at the night sky and studied the stars. They mapped their positions and created drawings that formed constellations. How are constellations perceived outside our galaxy, the Milky Way?

The constellations we see are based on Earth's perspective. Scientists can accurately determine the location of astronomical objects by using concepts like right ascension (RA), declination (Dec), and parallax as coordinates. Now, imagine traveling to an exoplanet! How would the positions of the stars appear differently from that vantage point?
